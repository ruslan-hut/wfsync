Business vs consumer. An order is treated as business when the buyer is in a known B2B category in the shop, or when they provided an EU VAT number, or when wFirma already has a VAT number saved for them.
Rate source. The shop sends the destination-country rate it calculated. We re-check it against our own EU rate database — the internal value wins if they disagree, and a warning is logged.
OSS evidence. EU consumer sales outside Poland are issued under the OSS scheme and carry two pieces of evidence about the buyer's country (billing address + delivery country).
VAT-number check. If a VAT number is present, it is verified against the EU VIES service. The check is non-blocking — a failure is logged for review but the invoice is still issued.
Reference document for the accounting and operations team — describes the rules the WFSync service applies before sending an invoice to wFirma.
